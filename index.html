Incident Log

Real incidents, real resolutions

A selection of production incidents I owned or played a key role in at Cognizant. Details are generalized to respect client confidentiality — happy to walk through any of them in depth.

Middleware

Traced the failure to a stale file lock on NAS storage backing the queue manager, coordinated with storage teams, and restored messaging safely without data loss. Middleware incidents often live below the middleware — knowing the storage and OS layer is what closes P1s.

INC-MQ-NASRESOLVED

WebSphere

Analyzed heap dumps and verbose GC logs, identified the memory pressure pattern, and drove the fix with the app team along with heap tuning to stabilize the JVM. Restarting buys minutes; reading the heap dump buys a permanent fix.

INC-JVM-OOMRESOLVED

CI/CD & Automation

Troubleshot the Jenkins build logs to isolate where the pipeline was failing, fixed the underlying issue on the server, and confirmed the pipeline ran cleanly end-to-end again. A broken pipeline is still a production incident — it just fails silently until someone needs a deployment to go out.

INC-JENKINS-PIPERESOLVED

Linux

Used top/ps and process-level analysis to identify the runaway process, worked with the app team to address the underlying cause, and confirmed CPU and response times returned to normal. Resource exhaustion incidents usually have a single runaway process behind them — finding it fast is the whole job.

INC-CPU-HIGHRESOLVED

Cleared archived logs to free space, restarted the affected services, and set a proactive disk-usage alert threshold to catch it earlier next time. The fix is five minutes; the monitoring threshold is what stops it from becoming a recurring page.

INC-DISK-FULLRESOLVED

Security

Identified the expired cert in the chain, coordinated renewal and keystore updates, and restored connectivity. Followed up with tracking so expiries were caught before they became incidents. The best P1 is the one your monitoring catches thirty days early.

INC-SSL-EXPRESOLVED

Release Management

Executed the pre-planned rollback, validated services were restored to the last known good state, coordinated with the development team on the root cause, and opened an RCA for follow-up. A deployment without a tested rollback plan isn't really a deployment plan — it's a gamble with an extra step.

INC-DEPLOY-RBRESOLVED

Prepared the change and rollback plan on short notice, got emergency approval, executed the deployment, and validated the fix before closing out the change. Emergency changes still need a rollback plan — "urgent" is not a substitute for "tested."

CHG-EMERGCOMPLETED

Operations

Investigated the listener and connection pool state, restarted the affected services, and validated that transactions resumed processing normally. Connection pool exhaustion is a symptom — the real question is what stopped releasing connections upstream.

INC-DB-CONNRESOLVED

Traced the alert through APM transaction traces to isolate the slow component, ruled out a false positive, and worked with the app team on the underlying fix. Good monitoring produces alerts you have to investigate, not just ones you have to silence.

INC-APM-ALERTRESOLVED

Identified a thread pool bottleneck through monitoring and thread dump analysis, adjusted JVM/thread pool configuration, and validated response times returned to normal. Not every incident is down/up — slow is still an incident, and it's often the harder one to diagnose.

INC-PERF-DEGRRESOLVED

Monitored application failover and database synchronization during the exercise, supported business validation of recovered services, and confirmed recovery met expected timelines. DR only works if it's rehearsed — the exercise is where you find the gaps the runbook didn't mention.

DR-FAILOVERCOMPLETED

Planned sequencing and rollback for each change, executed methodically with verification at every step, and completed all eight without incident. Separately caught a near double-deployment before it hit production. Checklists and rollback plans are what let one person safely do the work of a team.

CHG-X8-SOLOCOMPLETED
