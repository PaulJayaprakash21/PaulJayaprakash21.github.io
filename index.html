Incident Log

Real incidents, real resolutions

A selection of production incidents I owned or played a key role in at Cognizant. Details are generalized to respect client confidentiality — happy to walk through any of them in depth.

SEV 1 INC-MQ-NAS ✓ RESOLVED

MQ outage caused by NAS file lock

Impact
Queue manager failure disrupting message flow for banking applications.
Action
Traced the failure to a stale file lock on NAS storage backing the queue manager, coordinated with storage teams, and restored messaging safely without data loss.
Takeaway
Middleware incidents often live below the middleware — knowing the storage and OS layer is what closes P1s.
SEV 1 INC-JVM-OOM ✓ RESOLVED

Application outage from JVM OutOfMemory

Impact
WebSphere application server crashing under load, affecting a customer-facing banking service.
Action
Analyzed heap dumps and verbose GC logs, identified the memory pressure pattern, and drove the fix with the app team along with heap tuning to stabilize the JVM.
Takeaway
Restarting buys minutes; reading the heap dump buys a permanent fix.
SEV 1 INC-SSL-EXP ✓ RESOLVED

Service failure from expired SSL certificate

Impact
Secure connections failing between application components after a certificate expired.
Action
Identified the expired cert in the chain, coordinated renewal and keystore updates, and restored connectivity. Followed up with tracking so expiries were caught before they became incidents.
Takeaway
The best P1 is the one your monitoring catches thirty days early.
CHANGE CHG-X8-SOLO ✓ COMPLETED

Eight critical changes executed solo in one window

Context
A change window where I was the sole engineer responsible for executing eight critical production changes.
Action
Planned sequencing and rollback for each change, executed methodically with verification at every step, and completed all eight without incident. Separately caught a near double-deployment before it hit production.
Takeaway
Checklists and rollback plans are what let one person safely do the work of a team.
